## §1 · The harvest (what the research says, garden-sized)

1. **A garden of staggered visible timers is a real skill game** (Wildfrost) — the play is
   keeping the web deliberately out of phase and answering each timer at its moment.
2. **Allocation is the gardener's greed** (PvZ): economy-plants vs answer-plants — invest or
   answer NOW. A healer with an invest-early economy has a decision the Well never offers.
3. **Seats are lanes** (PvZ/Tapper): a per-ally row where danger races your growth makes the
   proactive-healer read legible — "does it ripen before the hit lands?"
4. **Automation + player-directed exceptions = a build game** (StS Defect): a garden that grows
   alone is allowed IF the presses visibly dominate outcomes (class rule 4 is the bar).
5. **Two half-games make mush** (Atomicrops): every candidate below is ONE instrument.
6. **The old kit's best idea was always RIPEN** — harvest-timing was the fun part of the frozen
   class; three of four candidates promote timing-the-garden to the whole game.

## §2 · THE CANDIDATES (pick ONE core; each lists its two spec seeds)

### CORE A — THE ORCHARD CLOCK · *keep the arcs staggered, catch every peak*

**The twist:** every Growth you plant is a visible RIPENESS ARC on the instrument (swell → RIPE
window → wilt). The graded press is the HARVEST — Bullseye at the peak pays the full heal +
Bloom; early sips small; wilted is lost. With 3–4 arcs live, the real game is **phase
management**: planting so ripe windows QUEUE instead of colliding, then catching each peak.
Wildfrost's timer-web on our graded-press grammar; the old RIPEN promoted to the whole game.
**What you're for:** the scheduler. You heal the FUTURE — a well-kept orchard drops peaks
exactly when the fight's damage lands (authored streams make that a plannable read).
**Dials:** arc speed · ripe width · stagger (self-set, the skill) · harvest grade · Sap. New
buttons: PLANT + HARVEST (2) — inside the narrow-kit budget.
**Example cards:** *Long Summer* (STRAT) — arcs 20% slower, ripe windows 20% wider (the tempo
creed-shape) · *Forced Bloom* (GREED) — harvest an UNRIPE arc at full value once per plant-cycle,
its next arc wilts 30% faster · *Second Fruit* (RULE) — a Bullseye harvest replants itself free.
**Spectacle keystone:** **FULL BLOOM** — land three Bullseye harvests inside one boss telegraph
cycle and the whole garden ripens AT ONCE: one chord-press harvests everything, the field erupts
in petals, the warband drinks the surplus.
**Spec seeds:** **ORCHARD** (few, slow, huge fruit — peak-catching precision) vs **MEADOW**
(many small fast blooms — density juggling; the Flourish lineage).
**Greed/comfort:** stagger-greed (running more arcs than you can cleanly catch); comfort = fewer
arcs, wider peaks. **AI:** a harvest-scheduler policy (threshold timing) — 3 tiers natural.
**Sources:** Wildfrost counters · old Wildgrove RIPEN · WoW resto blanket-weaving.

### CORE B — THE TRELLIS · *four lanes, and the damage is racing you*

**The twist:** the four seats are four LANES on the instrument. Sown seeds GROW along a lane
toward their ally; incoming authored damage shows as a spike racing down the same lane — the
core read is **the race**: a heal that arrives ripe beats the hit; late means triage. The graded
press is the DELIVERY at the lane's threshold. Underneath: the PvZ economy — some seeds are
SAP-makers (economy), some are heals (spenders); invest-vs-answer every sow.
**What you're for:** the logistician. You see the whole warband's next six seconds as freight on
rails and you make everything arrive on time.
**Dials:** growth speed · the race margin · delivery grade · the sap split · lane pressure. New
buttons: SOW + DELIVER (2).
**Example cards:** *Sunward Rows* (STRAT) — sap-seeds mature 30% faster (the invest creed-shape)
· *Photosynthesis* (GREED) — a delivery landed EXACTLY as the hit lands (the photo-finish) pays
150%, a lost race pays 50% · *Grafted Lines* (RULE) — a ripe undelivered heal may be re-routed
one lane over at 70%.
**Spectacle keystone:** **THE HARVEST TRAIN** — win three photo-finish races in a row and every
lane fruits simultaneously: one press delivers the whole board, the lanes flood green.
**Spec seeds:** **SOWER** (economy-heavy ramp — the sap engine) vs **COURIER** (race-heavy
snap deliveries — the clutch line).
**Greed/comfort:** margin-greed (sowing late for bigger, riskier deliveries); comfort = early
sows, fat margins. **AI:** ETA-vs-spike arithmetic — the most policy-legible core here.
**Skeptic fix folded:** lane-rendering cost flagged honestly (the richest HUD lift of the four —
it needs the per-seat rows the raid frame already draws, plus race markers).
**Sources:** PvZ lanes/sun · Tapper · the §1c peel-warning idiom (danger telegraphed per seat).

### CORE C — THE BRIAR · *armor that grows; the answer is planted early*

**The twist:** the DEFENSE garden. You plant briars ON allies; a briar ARMS over time (visible
arming arc — deeper = stronger); when its ally is hit, the briar ANSWERS (absorbs + snaps back)
at the strength it reached — and your graded press at the moment of the hit is the SNAP that
cashes it (the old Thornveil streak, made the core). Un-pressed briars still absorb weakly;
the press is where the healer's skill lives.
**What you're for:** the fortifier. You read the boss's authored stream and pre-arm the right
seat — proactive defense as prophecy, then a reflex cash when it lands.
**Dials:** arming depth · the snap grade · briar count · coverage choice (who's planted). New
buttons: PLANT + SNAP (2).
**Example cards:** *Ironbark* (STRAT) — arming continues 2s past full into an over-armed sliver
(deeper peak, narrower snap) · *Thorned Tithe* (GREED) — a Bullseye snap converts 30% of the
absorb into a boss-facing chip · *Rootbound Vow* (RULE) — your briar on the aggro-holder never
fully decays (the tank duet card).
**Spectacle keystone:** **THE HEDGE** — three Bullseye snaps in one fight grow THE HEDGE on the
whole warband: one telegraph string later, every seat's next eaten hit is answered in thorns at
once — the raid frame turns briar-green.
**Spec seeds:** **BRIAR** (wards/reflect — the Thornveil heir) vs **BALM** (the same arcs
planted as heals — a lighter Core-A hybrid).
**Greed/comfort:** depth-greed (arming past full); comfort = shallow arm, wide snap. **AI:**
arm-depth vs incoming-forecast thresholds; snap = the same timed-answer policies every kit runs.
**Skeptic fix folded:** bare-kit thinness — base kit ships briar + one direct graft-heal so zone
fights aren't ward-solitaire. Nearest-neighbor stated: the Well's wards are CAST reactions; the
briar is PLANTED prophecy — and the healer never self-heals the tank's job away (absorb ≠ heal).
**Sources:** Thornveil SNAP · AtO Bree thorns · LoP guard-regain (the answered-hit-pays shape).

### CORE D — THE PRUNING · *it grows wild; you cut it into shape* (the novel end)

**The twist:** the garden grows ITSELF — vines auto-spread across the warband frame, ticking
small heals (StS-Defect-style automation). Left alone it overgrows: wasted overheal, tangled
lanes, a sluggish field. Your presses are CUTS — graded prunes that redirect overgrowth into
the seat that needs it, harvest tangles into Sap, and shape where the wild growth goes next.
Healing by SUBTRACTION: the only kit in the roster where the press takes away.
**What you're for:** the editor of a living system. The garden is your co-pilot with bad
judgment; your shears are the judgment.
**Dials:** growth rate (the wildness) · tangle pressure · cut grade · redirect targeting · Sap.
New buttons: CUT + SHAPE (2).
**Example cards:** *Feral Rows* (GREED) — the garden grows 40% faster and tangles 40% faster:
more material, more mess · *Espalier* (STRAT) — your last cut leaves a trellis line; growth
follows it for 4s (steering as a card) · *Deadhead* (RULE) — pruning a tangle refunds Sap and
the next auto-tick doubles.
**Spectacle keystone:** **TOPIARY OF WAR** — five clean cuts without a tangle sculpt the garden
into a beast-shape that stalks the warband frame, eating incoming chip damage for 6s.
**Spec seeds:** **TOPIARY** (precision cuts, minimal wildness) vs **WILDWOOD** (maximum growth
rate, chaos-harvesting).
**Greed/comfort:** wildness-greed (cranking growth past what you can groom); comfort = slow
tidy garden. **AI:** a prune-priority ranking policy — expressible, but the honest hardest of
the four (flagged).
**⚠ Honesty note (skeptics):** automation cores flirt with rule 4 ("skill must move outcomes")
— this one survives ONLY because ungroomed overgrowth actively wastes and tangles, so expert
shears visibly dominate sloppy ones in the sim bands. If the gradient doesn't open in the base
tester, this core dies at the tester stage, stated now.
**Sources:** StS Defect (automation + exceptions) · Atomicrops animals (automation relief) ·
the Seedfall stacking lineage.
