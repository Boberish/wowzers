## 1. THE PREMISE (draft v1 — "the Return")

> **The Gilded Age:** generations ago the world was brilliant. People built WONDERS — a
> harvest that reaped itself, an engine that thought, a choir that never needed singers,
> a legion that never slept. Humans being humans, every wonder was pushed one notch too
> far, and one by one the wonders stopped being *ours*.
>
> **The Binding:** the wonders could not be killed. The makers' generation spent
> everything they had learning that — and then learning the thing that *did* work:
> binding. Under every wonder they closed a SEAL, swore the vigil, and locked the
> brilliant age underground.
>
> **The Quiet:** and here's the thing — **it worked.** The world didn't end. It got
> *smaller*, and then it got comfortable. Farms farm. Towns bicker. The sealed doors
> became scary stories, then landmarks, then furniture. The vigil greyed out politely.
>
> **The Return (now — the game):** a new generation grew up on those stories and drew
> the opposite conclusion: *all of the best things our people ever made are down there,
> still humming.* Not a war. A GOLD RUSH — on our own grandparents' locked estate.
> Warbands sign contracts, break the little seals, dive the domains, and haul the Gilded
> Age back out — and the deepest doors hold the wonders themselves, still awake, still
> polite, still absolutely certain they know best.

**Why this frame (maps to Bill's steers):**
- **Not post-apocalyptic.** The Binding *succeeded* — that's the whole reason the game
  gets to be cheerful. No ash, no scarcity, no survivor-grimness. The horror is contained,
  literally. Banned framings: wasteland biomes as default, survival economy, the word
  "apocalypse" anywhere in the fiction.
- **Fresh journey, chosen.** Nobody is drafted, nothing is invading. The Return is
  appetite, curiosity, and glory — a generation *deciding* it's time. The player is a
  prospector-knight on day one of a new profession, not a survivor in an aftermath.
- **No rift, no invaders — "humans being humans."** The monsters are OURS. Every Seal is
  a human ambition that worked too well. This kills the played-out portal/invasion trope
  AND gives the AI raid a thematic home: the thinking engine is just *one wonder among
  wonders* (§2). The joke stays contained and gains resonance.
- **Loot is diegetic.** Boons/curios/tokens = what the wonders still produce. Contracts
  exist because the dives are *worth money*. The run economy stops being gamey abstraction
  and becomes the point of the profession.

**The post-AI dial (Bill floated it, undecided):** three settings —
- **(a) MADE WONDERS (recommended):** wonders of every genre — alchemical, mechanical,
  musical, martial, computational. The machine mind is one of them. World stays varied;
  Realm 1's register stays special.
- **(b) FULL POST-AI:** every Seal is a machine mind. Maximum lean-in, but it globalizes
  the exact flavor we're trying to contain, makes the world single-note, and turns Realm
  1 from the crown-jewel joke into "another one."
- **(c) AMBIGUOUS:** nobody alive knows what the wonders were. Mystery-as-content.
- **Recommended blend: (a) is the truth, (c) is the surface.** The Quiet half-forgot;
  folk tales disagree with vigil records; *finding out what your grandparents actually
  built* is the campaign's story spine, uncovered door by door. Lore stays optional depth
  (clarity law §4: the surface story is one sentence — "the best things ever made are
  sealed under the world; go get them").

## 2. THE COHESION RULE — a region wears its Seal

One law generates all zone variety with one cause: **whatever is bound under a region
rewrites the land above it in its own image.** Every zone answers the same question —
*what's down there?* — with a different genre of wonder. Cohesion AND flavor freedom.

- **Zones (surface, conquered once):** the wonder's *leakage*. Gildfields = golden grain
  that reaps itself, husk-things walking the rows (the existing palette — HUSKMAN REAPER,
  the PALE TILLER — already reads exactly like this).
- **Dungeons/raids (under the doors, re-run forever):** the wonder's *domain* — its
  reality, rules, aesthetic, and sense of humor. Inside a door, anything goes (WORLD-PLAN's
  locked line stands: *jokes live inside realm doors, the world outside them is earnest…
  chipper* — see §3).
- **The re-run, explained:** a domain re-deals itself every time you cross its threshold —
  the wonder rebuilds its defense (seeded runs = the deal). Domain gifts don't survive the
  crossing (**Overworld Power Rule = fiction now**: the wonder's word only holds inside its
  walls). The surface is real and stays saved; ZONE REMEMBERS is literal.
- **Depth dial = the wonder's rules taking deeper hold.** Versions dial = the domain
  *revising its terms* (world-neutral name owed — coordinate with DUNGEON-PLAN's DOOR
  CONTRACT, §4).

**Seal seeds (riff fodder, nothing claimed):**
| Under it | The wonder (a human ambition, one notch too far) | Above it |
|---|---|---|
| the Undergranary (Dungeon 1) | **THE PLENTY** — a harvest that never needed farmers | the Gildfields: gold grain, husk reapers, granaries still filling |
| Realm 1 — THE TAKEOVER | **the thinking engine** — we built something to answer every question; it did | The Stack, exactly as written in the Realm 1 bible — untouched |
| the Mirefen (name already parked) | **THE IRRIGATOR** — weather/water tamed; now the rain won't stop where it drowned | drowned fen, patient water |
| a border zone | **THE STANDING ARMY** — soldiers that never sleep, a war that never demobilized | trench-garden battlefields grown over, still patrolled |
| a choir/bell zone | **THE EVERSONG** — music that performs itself | a county that hums; you don't whistle there |
| a garden realm (Bloomweaver energy) | **THE ARBOR** — a cultivar that gardens *us* | orchard-cathedral, root-streets |

⚠ **Vorathek re-skin owed:** currently billed "the rift-beast tutorial Seal" — needs a
re-hang on this frame (first-bound wonder? the beast the Binding was invented on?). Display

## 3. THE TONE LAW — heavy history, chipper present

**The register in one line: the HISTORY is heavy; the MOOD is light.** The Binding is
played dead straight and so is every fight; the *people* are chipper, ironic, jokey —
because for them the stakes are payday and glory, not extinction. Soldiers' humor, not
sitcom humor.

**Always straight (never the joke):** combat — telegraphs, strings, deaths, tuning
(generalizes Realm 1's locked rule world-wide) · the Binding and what it cost · the moment
any seal actually breaks · the wonders themselves when they finally speak (whatever their
register — Realm 1's politeness IS its menace).

**Where the light lives:** dialogue · ticket/contract flavor text · shopkeepers ·
end-screens · warband banter (the Realm-1 ally-banter idea generalizes: view-only, never
in the checksum). The world outside doors is *earnest* (WORLD-PLAN's word) — earnest
doesn't mean grim; it means the jokes are the people's, never the narrator's.

**Not all static (Bill):** the cast has REGISTERS — anchors who bring it down, sparks who
bring it up, and the mix is the texture. Archetype seeds:
- **The clerk** (Bastion contract board, spark): *"Sign here, initial here. Deposit's
  non-refundable in the event of — well. Lovely weather for it!"*
- **The last vigil-keeper** (anchor): *"We didn't seal them because we won. We sealed them
  because it was the only thing left that worked. Mind you hold the line at the threshold."*
- **The homesteader kid** (spark): wants your autograph; grandfather *built* part of the
  Plenty and the family is quietly, deeply embarrassed.
- **A wonder's leftover servitor** (register-breaker): utterly loyal to a master it can no
  longer find; comic AND heartbreaking, both at once.

## 4. THE NAMING LAW — clarity beats lore (the reskin answer)

Bill's worry: dynamic renames = confusion. The answer: **we don't do dynamic renames.**

1. **SYSTEM NOUNS get ONE global name, forever, everywhere.** Rarity tier, curse, shop,
   ticket, token, creed, module, dodge, kick. The UI never relabels a mechanic by location.
2. **CONTENT NOUNS wear costumes.** Proper names — shops, bosses, curse *families*, cards,
   curios — are themed per zone/realm. A run-shop *named* "PROMPT MARKET" inside Realm 1 is
   a shop named Bob's: the UI still says Shop, players already expect every MMO district to
   name its own vendors. **JAILBREAK = Realm 1's curse family; the mechanic is "curse."**
   This is the pattern — PROMPT MARKET and JAILBREAK were already doing it right.
3. **VISUAL GRAMMAR never changes.** Rarity colors/frames, curse iconography, node glyphs —
   identical in every zone and realm, whatever the local proper nouns say. A player who
   can't read still knows what everything is.

**Consequences (the leak audit, triaged):**
| Surface | Today | Ruling under the law |
|---|---|---|
| Rarity tiers | `haiku/sonnet/opus` — system noun in Realm-1 costume, engine-wide (`draft.gd`) | **Violation — the big one.** Pick world-neutral names once (§6.3); Realm 1 keeps model-name jokes in *card/content names*, not the tier label. Display-layer first; id sweep later as one mechanical commit (rename via display fields, never ids — sims stay byte-identical, MASTER's acceptance bar). |
| TOKENS | global currency + "spend them responsibly" gag | **Word survives** (tokens are coin-neutral, and the wonders *minting scrip* is good fiction); the gag line goes Realm-1-local. |
| Feints = HALLUCINATIONS ("canonical, everywhere") | MASTER §REALMS | Realm-1 register only. World-neutral word: **feint**. |
| Trial ladder = model VERSIONS + fake patch notes | MASTER §REALMS / DUNGEON door | Realm-1 costume of a global dial. Neutral name owed for the DOOR CONTRACT dial (candidates: TERMS · EDITIONS · the door "asks harder terms"). Realm 1 keeps patch notes inside its door. |
| Creeds | class-wide | **Native.** A creed is sworn belief — binding-words language. Keep. |
| Modules | class-wide | Reads machine-flavored. Candidates: VOW · CLAUSE · RITE · TENET — or keep (players tolerate gamey menu words). Design docs keep saying "module" regardless (internal vocabulary ≠ display, same split as ids). §6.4. |
| CURIO ARMORY v3 names (Prompt Injection · Golden GPU · Malware Miner…) | GEAR-CATALOG §ARMORY-v3, at narrowing verdict | Curios are *global* items → world-neutral names for keeps; the AI-flavored jokes either re-skin or become Realm-1 drop-scoped. **Fold into the armory narrowing verdict** — flag added there. |

## 5. WHAT STAYS · WHAT BENDS (reconciliation)

**Stays, gains meaning:** SEALS (now literal — bound, not slain; the raid noun becomes the
premise) · the BASTION (the vigil's fortress → the Return's boomtown) · descent/Depth
structure (down is where the wonders are) · OATHS/CREEDS/TICKETS/LEDGER (binding-words
vocabulary was already everywhere) · Gildfields palette · Realm 1 bible, entire, inside
its door.

**Bends:** the RIFT (origin + word retired from fiction; realms = domains *under* seals,
not tears into elsewhere; game title open §6.5) · global Haiku/Sonnet/Opus + tokens-gag
(→ Realm-1, §4) · "solo classes keep the dark-fantasy Rift identity" (MASTER §REALMS — the
world identity is now THIS doc) · Vorathek's rift-beast billing (§2 note).
