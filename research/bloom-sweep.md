## The finds

1. **Plants vs Zombies — the sun economy + lanes.** The core tension is ALLOCATION: economy
   plants (sunflowers) vs answer plants (peashooters) — spend on immediate utility or long-term
   revenue. PvZ deliberately priced sunflowers cheap to teach investing. And the lane structure
   (Tapper heritage) makes multitasking spatial: threats race down rows toward what you protect.
   For a 4-seat warband: **the seats ARE four lanes.**
2. **Wildfrost — counter timers.** Every unit acts on its own visible countdown; play = managing
   a WEB of interlocking timers, with cards that advance/retard them. "Mindlessly playing never
   works — you calculate every move." The cleanest modern proof that **a garden of staggered
   timers is a real skill game**, not idle decoration.
3. **Atomicrops — tend-vs-fight, and its failure mode.** Farming mid-combat generates genuine
   multitask thrill, but critics converge on the same lesson: two half-games make mush —
   "the developers should have honed farming OR shooting." The steal is the WARNING: our garden
   must live on ONE instrument inside the one HUD, never become a second screen.

## STEAL CANDIDATES mapped to our grammar (Bloomweaver-flavored)

- **Wildfrost timers → ripeness arcs as the core instrument.** Growths with visible countdown
  arcs; the graded press = catching the RIPE window (the old Wildgrove RIPEN, promoted from
  aspect quirk to THE game). Cards nudge arcs (hasten/hold) = the Wildfrost item grammar.
- **PvZ allocation → a sap economy inside the healer kit.** Economy-seeds vs heal-seeds as a
  per-cast choice; teaches invest-early like the halved sunflower. (dials: the sow, the sap)
- **PvZ lanes → seats-as-lanes.** A per-ally growth row where incoming spikes RACE your
  growth to the ally — "does the heal ripen before the hit lands?" is a legible, dramatic read.
- **Atomicrops' warning → the one-instrument law for every candidate.** Any core that needs a
  second attention surface fails the slate before the skeptics even sit.
- **Automation done right (StS Defect kin) → gardens that grow alone, players who EDIT.** The
  Defect's orb queue proves automated value + player-directed exceptions = a build game; a
  pruning core (heal by cutting, not casting) is the novel end of the slate.
