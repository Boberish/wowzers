# Hades 1 + Hades 2 — Build & Boon Design (knowledge base)

Source game: Hades (Supergiant, 2020) and Hades II (Early Access 2024 → **1.0 shipped Sept 2025**).
Reference for Project Rift's draft economy. **We already took the obvious surface** — 1-of-3 boon picks,
god-pool routing legibility, pact-as-difficulty-dial. This file goes for the NEXT layer: how aspects
rewire movesets (= our sub-spec branches), how hammers/duos/Chaos/poms structure greed, and how
Hades 2's arcana/element/omega systems restructure the whole meta. Read section 6 for the steals.

---

## 1. Build grammar

**Hades 1 run stack:** pick a **weapon** (6 Infernal Arms) → pick an **aspect** (4 per weapon; the 4th
is a hidden "legendary" aspect that often *rewrites the moveset*) → enter the run and draft **boons**
from Olympian gods (1-of-3 offered per reward room, slotted onto Attack / Special / Cast / Dash / Call)
→ grab **Daedalus Hammer** upgrades (weapon-only moveset rule-changers, offered in dedicated rooms,
mutually exclusive, un-rerollable) → **poms of power** deepen an existing boon a level → **Chaos boons**
(curse-now, blessing-later) and **Hermes boons** (speed/utility, own pool). One **keepsake** equipped per
run (swappable between chambers) biases the pools. Meta layer = the **Mirror of Night** (flat unlockable
passives, two toggleable columns).

**Hades 2 changed almost every noun.** You now play **Melinoë** with a **Magick** resource that fuels
**Omega Moves** (hold Attack/Special/Cast/Sprint to spend Magick for a supercharged version of the move
you already have — no new button, the *held* release is the skill test). **Casts** now plant a circle
that traps foes. Boons carry a hidden **Element** (Earth/Water/Air/Fire/Aether); stacking enough of one
element auto-fires an **Infusion boon** (a cross-god synergy layer orthogonal to god identity). Selene
grants **Hexes** (one big charged super, upgraded mid-run along a **Path of Stars** mini-tree). The Mirror
is replaced by **Arcana Cards** — a pre-run tarot loadout you slot against a **Grasp** budget (0–5 per
card, cap ~10 rising to ~29), with **awakening/adjacency** bonuses. **Incantations** are permanent
cauldron rituals (meta crafting) that unlock features. Difficulty knob renamed **Fear** (the **Oath of the
Unseen**, Vows instead of the Pact of Punishment). Persistent progression uses reagents (Psyche, Ash, etc.).

## 2. Weapon / aspect roster (aspects = the closest thing in gaming to our sub-spec branches)

An **aspect is a card that rewires how the weapon's core move works** — sometimes a stat lean, sometimes a
*total moveset replacement*. The transformative ones are the model to study.

### Hades 1 — the 6 Infernal Arms (4 aspects each; 4th is hidden/legendary)

- **Stygius, the Stygian Blade** (longsword). *Zagreus* move/dash lean · *Nemesis* — for 3s after Special,
  Attacks can crit (up to +30% crit) → "special-then-swing" rhythm · *Poseidon* — Special knocks foes into
  walls for bonus · **Arthur (hidden)** — **new moveset**: slower, wide Holy Excalibur swings, **+50 max
  HP**, Special plants **Hallowed Ground** aura (foes deal less damage, projectiles slow, traps disable). A
  whole different tempo and defensive fantasy from one aspect.
- **Coronacht, the Heart-Seeking Bow** (charge-shot). *Zagreus* crit · *Chiron* — Special **auto-homes to
  the last enemy your Attack hit**, firing a multi-arrow volley → "Attack to mark, Special to auto-fire"
  build · *Hera* — Cast ammo loads into the next Attack and detonates on hit (Cast+bow fusion) · **Rama
  (hidden)** — Celestial Sharanga: Attack applies a **Hunter's Flare** mark, Special drops a stationary
  relentless volley; rewires bow into a mark-and-zone weapon.
- **Aegis, the Shield of Chaos** (block + Bull Rush). *Zagreus* flat DR · *Chaos* — Bull Rush throws
  **multiple shields** (spin-to-win) · *Zeus* — Special becomes a **Blitz Disc** that bounces between foes,
  pick it back up · **Beowulf (hidden)** — Naegling's Board: replaces Bull Rush with a **steerable Dragon
  Rush** charge, **+damage but +10% damage taken** (aggression tax; the "trade defense for offense" aspect).
- **Varatha, the Eternal Spear** (throw + spin). *Zagreus* range/speed · *Achilles* — retrieve the thrown
  spear as a **Raging Rush** dash for bonus damage · *Hades* — Spin becomes a huge **Punishing Sweep** ·
  **Guan Yu (hidden)** — Frost Fair Blade: **~halves max HP**, Spin lifesteals and gains area; a
  glass-cannon lifedrain rewrite.
- **Malphon, the Twin Fists** (fast combo). *Zagreus* dodge% · *Talos* — Special becomes a **Magnetic
  Cutter** that *pulls foes in* + attack bonus · *Demeter* — 12 landed strikes empower the next Special ·
  **Gilgamesh (hidden)** — Claws of Enkidu: Dash-strike becomes a **Dash-Upper that Maims** (foe takes AND
  deals more damage) — a debuff-carrier rewrite.
- **Exagryph, the Adamant Rail** (gun + reload). *Zagreus* ammo cap · *Eris* — walk into your own Special
  blast for an **8s damage buff** (risk-reward positioning) · *Hestia* — **manual reload empowers** the next
  shot (active-reload skill beat) · **Lucifer (hidden)** — Igneus Eden: Special becomes a **detonatable
  Hellfire orb** — a place-and-pop rewrite.

### Hades 2 — the 5 (→6) Nocturnal Arms (Aspect of Melinoë default + 2 named + 1 hidden each)

- **Witch's Staff** (ranged wand, the starter). *Melinoë* max-Magick/Special power · *Circe* — your **Animal
  Familiar casts alongside you** via a damaging Psychic Leash · *Momus* — **Omega Moves fire in place up to
  3× until re-used** (plant delayed bombs, then detonate — a planning rewrite) · **Anubis (hidden)** — Ankh
  Scepter, kills summon Lone Shades.
- **Sister Blades** (fast dual daggers). *Melinoë* backstab bonus · *Pan* — Specials **seek foes trapped in
  your Cast** and fire extra shots · *Artemis* — while channeling Omega Attack you **auto-Parry then
  Riposte** (defensive rhythm added to offense) · **Morrigan (hidden)** — Crow Cutters, Blood Triad ritual.
- **Umbral Flames** (mid-range flails). *Melinoë* crit potential · *Moros* — **Attacks linger 6s and explode
  when struck by your Specials** (set-up/pay-off combo) · *Eos* — Omega Attack fires a **Daybreaker DoT
  zone** · **Supay (hidden)** — Devil Sparks boosts Rush boons.
- **Moonstone Axe** (slow heavy). *Melinoë* attack power + max life · *Charon* — Cast erupts when hit by your
  Omega Special · *Thanatos* — faster attacks stacking **Mortality (a damage multiplier that only resets
  when you take damage** — pure greed, tied to not getting hit) · **Nergal (hidden)** — Rock Lion Mace,
  Berserk after enough hits.
- **Argent Skull** (shotgun, limited shells). *Melinoë* power per unspent shell · *Medea* — Attack sticks
  and explodes after a Special or 3s · *Persephone* — Special becomes **Sprouted** (steerable) **and boons
  start with bonus levels** (a rarity/power lean that reshapes the whole draft) · **Hel (hidden)** — Frost
  Mane, Valkyrie attack speed after Specials.
- **Black Coat** (melee/mid brawler; hidden 6th weapon). *Melinoë* movement/sprint speed · *Selene* — **start
  the run already holding a Hex** (Sky Fall) applying Shine · *Nyx* — Possess Boost spawns Nightspawn ·
  **Shiva (hidden)** — Purifying Grace absorbs Special blasts to grow Destructive.

**Design read:** aspects prove one card can change a kit's *timing shape and win-condition*, not just its
stats. Arthur/Guan Yu/Beowulf change the whole moveset; Chiron/Thanatos/Momus change one core move enough
to redefine the build. This is exactly the ceiling our branch **Modules** should reach.

## 3. Boon & upgrade design patterns

- **God = one coherent effect family + a personality.** Each Olympian owns a single, legible mechanic across
  every slot: Zeus = chain **lightning** (splash/greed), Poseidon = **knockback + wealth** (safety + loot),
  Aphrodite = **Weak** (defensive debuff), Ares = **Doom** burst (delayed greed), Athena = **Deflect**
  (parry/safety), Artemis = **crit** (single-target), Demeter = **Chill/Freeze** (control), Dionysus =
  **Hangover** DoT (stacking), Hermes = speed/utility. You read a god's *whole* identity from one boon —
  legibility we already borrowed.
- **Slot system = our dial-lanes.** Boons attach to **Attack / Special / Cast / Dash / Call**. Each slot is
  a distinct part of the moveset, so drafting is "which part of my kit does this god improve," not a flat
  stat pile. Dash-boons (defensive/utility) and Call-boons (the meter-charged super) are separate lanes.
- **Daedalus Hammers = the RULE vs POWER split, made a dedicated reward.** Hammers are **weapon-only
  moveset rule-changers** (Triple Shot: bow fires a 3-spread; Dashing Flight: dash empowers the shield
  throw), offered in *their own rooms*, usually 1-of-2/3, **mutually exclusive, and un-rerollable**. Some
  carry **real downsides** (Explosive Shot: +300% area but **+100% charge time**) — a rule change you must
  then play *around*. This is a cleaner RULE/POWER separation than most roguelikes: stat boons widen, hammers
  *rewrite*.
- **Poms of Power = vertical vs horizontal.** A Pom doesn't give a new boon — it **raises an existing boon's
  level**, scaling its numbers. Every Pom is a "deepen what I have vs. reach for something new" decision, and
  some boons only get *good* at high level (Ares Doom, Dionysus Hangover).
- **Duo boons = capstones gated on TWO gods.** A Duo requires you to already hold specific prerequisite
  boons from *both* gods (e.g. **Merciful End** = Ares + Athena: Deflect triggers Doom; **Cold Fusion** =
  Zeus + Poseidon; **Lightning Rod** = Zeus + Artemis: marked foes get zapped; **Curse of Drowning** = Ares
  + Poseidon). They are the build's payoff *and* its proof — you only see them if you committed to a pair.
- **Legendary boons = mono-god loyalty payoff.** One per god, gated behind a prerequisite chain (e.g. Zeus
  **Splitting Bolt** needs Storm Lightning + Double Strike/High Voltage; Ares **Vicious Cycle** needs a Blade
  Rift boon). Reward for going *deep* in one lane, opposite pull to the Duo's *two-lane* pull.
- **Chaos boons = curse-now-pay-off-later greed.** Enter a Chaos gate at a small **HP cost**; you take a
  **Curse penalty** (−dash, −attack, less HP…) for the next N encounters, after which it flips into a
  **permanent run Blessing** (+damage, +cast size…). Legible risk you schedule against your route.
- **God keepsakes = routing control.** Equipping a god's keepsake **guarantees that god's symbol appears at
  the next Olympian door** — you *steer the offered pool*. Swappable between chambers, so you route into the
  boons your build wants, then swap to a combat keepsake. Other keepsakes: Lucky Tooth (extra Death Defiance
  = revive), Cosmic Egg, etc.
- **Hades 2 additions:** **Elements/Infusions** — every non-infusion boon carries a hidden element; hit an
  element threshold and an **Infusion boon auto-activates** (cross-god synergy that rewards *thematic*
  drafting without an explicit combo). **Omega Moves** — a universal "spend Magick, hold, release" verb that
  super-sizes any core move. **Hexes** — one big Selene super charged on its own meter and upgraded mid-run
  along the **Path of Stars**. Boon **rarity** ladder (Common→Rare→Epic→**Heroic**→Legendary/Duo) means the
  *same* boon can be rolled bigger — a second axis of "deepen vs widen."

## 4. Difficulty & self-tuning systems

**Hades 1 — Pact of Punishment / Heat.** After the first clear you build **Heat** by stacking **~16
independent conditions**, each with graded ranks. Every point of Heat = harder run; specific Heat totals
gate **Bounties** (one-time reward drops). It's *player-authored difficulty for legible reward*. Notable
knobs and why players pick them:
- **Hard Labor** (+20% enemy damage / rank, max +5) and **Lasting Consequences** (less healing) — the "flat
  danger" knobs; simple, so common for easy early Heat.
- **Tight Deadline** (9:00 region timer, tightening; up to +10 Heat) — the *skill* knob speedrunners love;
  turns the run into a DPS check.
- **Extreme Measures** (+1 Heat/rank, up to 10) — **doesn't inflate numbers, gives bosses NEW mechanics/
  phases**. The most respected knob: harder *fights*, not bigger *sponges*.
- **Middle Management** (+1 elite per miniboss), **Convenience Fee** (shop prices up), **Jury Summons**
  (more foes), **Forced Overtime** (foes faster/enraged), **Heightened Security** (traps), **Approval
  Process** (fewer boon choices), **Personal Liability** (+damage taken).
- Players cherry-pick the *cheap-for-them* knobs (economy/shop knobs if they don't shop) and avoid the ones
  that fight their build — Heat is a **self-tuning dial where each player finds their own comfortable path up**.
  Max ~63 Heat (64 Hell Mode).

**Hades 2 — Fear / Oath of the Unseen.** Same idea, renamed and re-themed (sworn to Nyx). Activate **Vows**
to raise **Fear**; total ~55 Fear across all vows. Examples: **Vow of Pain** (+20/60/100% enemy damage by
rank), **Vow of Rivals** (the Extreme-Measures heir: fight **Unrivaled** boss versions with *new mechanics*,
in fixed order, no skipping), **Vow of Forsaken/Nemeses/Void** etc. Fear gates **Testaments** (per-weapon
reward tiers at 1/2/4/8/12/16/20/24 Fear — **non-linear**, so early Fear is cheap and deep Fear is a real
commitment). Same design DNA: **difficulty is a currency you spend to buy rewards.**

**Comfort direction — God Mode.** The opposite dial: an opt-in assist granting **+20% damage resistance,
rising +2% per death up to 80%** — it *auto-ramps as you struggle*, so the game meets a stuck player without
a menu of sliders. Toggleable any time, no penalty. The key insight: **difficulty is bidirectional and
self-adjusting** — Heat pushes up for reward, God Mode drifts up (in defense) automatically to keep you in.

## 5. Party / co-op interplay (Hades has none — this is our opening)

Hades is strictly single-player, so its entire boon economy assumes ONE build. The ideas that only get
interesting with our **4-seat warband**:
- **Duo boons across PLAYERS, not gods.** A capstone that activates only when two *different seats* each
  hold the prerequisite branch — Hades' two-god gate becomes a two-*teammate* gate. Composition is the lock.
- **Keepsake/routing as a team-draft protocol.** In Hades you steer your own pool; with 4 seats, one seat
  "calling" a god pulls it for the *table* — routing becomes social bargaining over a shared node.
- **Element/Infusion thresholds pooled across the warband** — the team collectively hits an element count,
  unlocking a raid-wide Infusion. Thematic drafting becomes a group project.

## 6. STEAL CANDIDATES for Rift

Filter applied: **borrow the grammar, innovate the sentence.** Already taken (do NOT repack): 1-of-3
drafts, god-routing legibility, pact-as-EASE. These exploit our **timing-minigame + deterministic engine**.

1. **Aspect-as-Module: the moveset-rewrite card.** Hades proves ONE card (Arthur/Momus/Chiron) can change a
   kit's *timing shape*, not just its numbers. Our branch **Module** should be allowed to do this — e.g. a
   Module that turns a minigame's single accelerating beat into a **two-speed polyrhythm**, or flips
   "tap-on-beat" into "hold-and-release." The Creed→Module→Boons→Keystone branch becomes a genuine
   sub-*spec* the way an aspect makes a genuine sub-weapon, not a stat tree.

2. **Hammer rooms: RULE cards in their own dedicated node, mutually exclusive, un-rerollable.** Separate the
   RULE-type reward from the boon draft — a rare "forge/rig" node offers 1-of-3 **moveset rule-changers**
   you can't reroll and can't stack against each other. Because our engine is deterministic, a rule change
   (e.g. "your Perfect window now also emits on the beat *before*") composes cleanly and is testable.

3. **Downside RULE cards (Explosive Shot pattern).** A RULE card that is a *real* tradeoff you must time
   around: e.g. "+40% finisher damage but your window is 30% tighter for two beats after each finisher." This
   is the **BITE direction of EASE made permanent and build-specific** — a knob you *chose* that reshapes how
   you play, not a difficulty menu. Deterministic ticks let the penalty window be exact.

4. **Chaos-boon time-greed on the minigame window.** Take a card that **tightens your Bullseye/Perfect
   window (curse) for the next 3 fights**, then permanently **widens Perfect→Bullseye conversion + damage**
   after. Our tick-count schedules the flip to the exact fight — legible greed you route around, and it bites
   *the timing skill itself*, which no stat-based Chaos boon can do.

5. **Cross-seat Duo capstones (the co-op innovation Hades couldn't make).** A Keystone-tier card that only
   arms when **two warband seats each hold the prerequisite branch** — e.g. Twinfang's Opening windows also
   trigger the Alchemist's poison Rupture, but only if both are seated. Composition-locked spectacle. Maps to
   our Keystone slot; the prerequisite is *another player's build*, not a second god.

6. **Loyalty payoff vs. pair payoff (Legendary vs. Duo tension).** Give every branch a **mono-branch
   Legendary**: draft N boons from one dial-lane and unlock its Keystone as a *guaranteed* offer — a second
   path to the Keystone that rewards *going deep* in one branch, pulling against the cross-branch/duo pull.
   Two opposite greed vectors keeps drafting tense.

7. **Poms = a "sharpen" currency (vertical vs horizontal at every node).** A resource that **raises the
   grade payoff of an already-drafted boon** (a Good-tier bonus becomes a Perfect-tier bonus) instead of
   adding a new card. Every node becomes "deepen what I have vs. reach wider," and some boons only get scary
   when sharpened — exactly the Ares-Doom-only-good-at-high-level texture.

8. **Element/Infusion hidden tags across branches.** Tag every Rift boon with a hidden **tempo-tag**
   (SPEED / BURST / GUARD). Accumulate a threshold of one tag across your drafted cards → an **Infusion card
   auto-activates**, addressing the minigame at the system level (e.g. GUARD×4 → every dodge also refunds a
   beat). Rewards *thematic* drafting across branches without a hand-authored combo — Hades 2's cleanest new
   idea, and deterministic thresholds fire it predictably.

9. **Omega-hold for the signature CD (spend-meter-to-supersize, no new button).** Instead of one flat
   cooldown, a **Magick-like pool** that lets you *hold* an existing core beat into an **Omega-Perfect** for
   extra payoff — the hold-release is still a timing test, so it AMPLIFIES skill (never button=damage) and
   respects the ABILITY LAW (no new touch target, you overload a move you already have).

10. **Arcana-board pre-run loadout with adjacency awakenings (the meta layer, not the in-run draft).** Our
    persistent overworld's between-run screen = a **Grasp-budget board**: spend a budget across passive cards
    (each 0–5), where placing **two same-cost cards adjacent auto-awakens a free bonus**. Deeper than a flat
    unlock tree, it makes the *loadout itself* a puzzle, and it's fully deterministic to sim.

11. **"Rivals" hard-mode = new telegraph beats, not bigger numbers (Extreme Measures).** When a player slides
    EASE toward BITE at the *boss* level, add **authored telegraph beats / a new phase**, not stat inflation.
    Our single-telegraph-stream engine is built for this: BITE re-scores the boss's telegraph rhythm. This is
    the *respected* difficulty knob and it gates the best loot (see #12).

12. **Difficulty gates loot (Bounties/Testaments).** Certain Keystones/loot only drop when the EASE dial is
    at BITE with specific knobs set — self-set difficulty becomes the key to the best rewards, and the
    non-linear Testament curve (cheap early Fear, expensive deep Fear) is a good pacing template for how
    aggressively to price each BITE notch.

13. **Run-long "ultimate builder" (Hex Path of Stars) for the signature CD.** Let players invest earned
    charges *during a run* into **which facet of the 1-min moment amplifies** — wider window, more targets,
    longer Opening. The signature grows into the build you're actually assembling instead of being fixed at
    run start.

14. **Keepsake that biases the NEXT draft's dial.** A swappable slot that **guarantees the next draft
    addresses a chosen minigame dial** (routing toward the build you're assembling). Deterministic seeded
    pools let us *promise* a dial reliably — the Hades keepsake trick, aimed at our dial-lanes instead of gods.

15. **Mastery-through-use unlocks (keepsake affinity / aspect unlock).** Branch Keystones/aspects unlock by
    clearing content **with that branch equipped**, not by grinding a separate currency — progression comes
    from *playing the thing*, which is more legible and less grindy than a shop.

### Twinfang / rogue-flavored subsection (Flow 0–6, Bullseye/Perfect/Good, Eviscerate, Coup de Grâce, Opening ×1.9, one dodge)

- **Chiron-Module for Twinfang (attack-marks, finisher-fires).** A branch Module where **Eviscerate plants a
  mark**, and for the next few beats your taps **auto-home the rhythm to the marked target** — "combo to
  mark, finisher to auto-lock." Rewires target flow the way Aspect of Chiron rewires the bow. (Steal #1.)
- **Thanatos-Creed (Mortality greed on Flow).** A Creed where **consecutive Bullseyes stack a multiplier
  that only resets when you take a hit** — Flow becomes a no-hit greed meter, tying the reward directly to
  your **one spacebar dodge**. Pure "how long dare you stay clean." (Steals #4/#6/Thanatos aspect.)
- **Nemesis-card (dump opens a crit window).** Spending **Coup de Grâce** opens a **~3s window where taps can
  Bullseye-crit** — you *pay the dump to buy a burst window*, a self-made cousin of the boss **Opening ×1.9**.
  Rewards spending, not hoarding. (Steal: Stygius Aspect of Nemesis.)
- **Chaos-window greed.** A card that **tightens the Bullseye window for 3 fights**, then permanently widens
  **Perfect→Bullseye conversion** and adds damage — greed that bites the rhythm skill itself, scheduled to
  the exact fight by tick-count. (Steal #4.)
- **Omega-Eviscerate.** **Hold** the finisher input to spend **Flow** for a bigger Eviscerate — but the
  *release* must land in the window or it fizzles. Spend-meter-to-supersize with a skill gate; no new button.
  (Steal #9.)
- **Cross-seat Opening duo.** A Keystone that makes Twinfang's **Opening windows also apply the Alchemist's
  Rupture** — only live when both seats are in the warband. Composition-locked spectacle. (Steal #5.)
- **Infusion tempo-tags on the rogue deck.** Enough BURST-tagged boons → an Infusion that makes **every 6-Flow
  Eviscerate also refund one beat of Flow**, sustaining the loop without an explicit combo. (Steal #8.)
