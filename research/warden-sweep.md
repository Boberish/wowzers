## The finds

1. **Lies of P — perfect guard + Guard Regain.** Regular block converts part of the damage into
   a RECOVERABLE sliver you win back by ATTACKING soon after; perfect guard costs almost no
   stamina, stops everything, staggers, and even wears down the ENEMY's weapon. The loop:
   defense mints a short offense window, and sloppy defense literally hands your health economy
   to your aggression. Their "attack to reclaim" is Bloodborne's rally grafted onto block.
2. **Bloodborne — rally/regain.** A hit leaves an orange "potential health" segment (~5s);
   striking back refills it, heavier weapons refill more, a visceral refills it all. The design
   lesson: **the wound itself is a timer-resource** — a short window where counterplay converts
   loss into currency. (⚠ our law: the tank NEVER self-heals — any steal must convert the wound
   window into ◆/wind/damage, not HP; the healer owns the refill.)
3. **Monster Hunter lance/gunlance — the guard-counter economy.** "Every hit the monster lands
   is a counter opportunity — the question isn't whether to block, but WHICH counter you fire
   back." Perfect guard = zero chip, no stagger; **Offensive Guard** pays +15% attack after each
   clean guard — constant-guarding IS the damage rotation. Lance = precise counter timing;
   gunlance = stored explosive punish. Two flavors of "defense chooses the offense."
4. **For Honor — Conqueror full-block + superior block.** Full-block stance eats everything from
   any direction; a SUPERIOR block acts like a parry — the attacker is pushed back, their chain
   is ABORTED, and you get a GUARANTEED unblockable follow-up. The steal-shape: a graded block
   that interrupts the enemy's string and mints a promised counter, not just mitigation.
5. **Vermintide 2 — stamina block/push economy.** Block and PUSH share one stamina pool; push
   spends stamina to stagger and buy space; block-cost-reduction is the tank's defining stat;
   one whole tanking style turns the stamina bar into the real HP bar (stagger generates temp
   HP). The steal-shape: the pool is the true health, and SPENDING defense proactively (push)
   is a move, not just holding.

## STEAL CANDIDATES mapped to our grammar (Warden-flavored)

- **Offensive Guard / guard-counter economy → a SLAM-chain theme.** Clean SHIELDs pay forward
  (next counter bigger / taps cheaper); the rotation is built ON the boss's bars. (dials: the
  slam, the bank)
- **Body-Slam-by-way-of-Return-to-Sender → a storage theme.** Prevented damage charges a battery
  hurled back on YOUR timing — the parked `Return to Sender` card is already this theme's seed;
  StS Barricade/Body-Slam proves the archetype carries a whole build. (dials: the hold, the spend)
- **Rally, legalized → wound-window plays.** A leaked sliver opens a short window where a clean
  answer banks ◆/wind ("answer the wound") — loss into currency, never into HP. (dials: the
  duet, the bank)
- **Superior-block chain-abort → keystone-grade spectacle.** A perfect SLAM that visibly shoves
  the NEXT bar back down the lane (the boss's string staggered on the same instrument). (keystone)
- **Stamina-as-real-HP / BCR → the endurance theme's spine.** Hold-drain efficiency, over-hold
  greed, winded brinkmanship — the big pool IS the Warden's health-before-health. (dials: the
  hold, the wind)
- **Push (spend defense to buy space) → a proactive wind-spend move-shape** for boons: pay wind
  NOW to blunt/delay what's coming, chosen per use — greed's comfort-side twin. (dials: the wind,
  the read)
